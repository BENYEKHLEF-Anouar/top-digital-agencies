How to evaluate digital agencies' SEO claims

Published June 23, 2026 By AMD Editorial Team 5 min read
Table of contents
1. Traffic audits 2. Backlink quality 3. Reporting parameters 4. Verification checklist

Most SEO pitches follow the same playbook: screenshots of massive traffic spikes, lists of highly commercial keywords, and templates of technical fixes. Without rigorous audit steps, clients struggle to separate organic authority from short-term manipulation.

To avoid spending budgets on short-lived rankings, organizations must audit case studies directly, verifying dates, tool sources, and organic traffic distribution.

1. Traffic audits

When an agency demonstrates a traffic graph showing 400% growth, always demand raw tool verification. A common trick is utilizing brand traffic growth. If a client runs a massive television campaign, organic brand search will spike. The agency will claim credit for this traffic, despite it being driven by offline media budgets.

Ensure you request graphs that explicitly exclude the brand name keywords. This reveals the actual organic growth driven by informational and non-branded commercial search queries.

"If an agency cannot isolate non-branded organic traffic in their historical case studies, they are either hiding poor performance or lack fundamental analytics skills."

2. Backlink quality

Quantity of backlinks is a vanity metric. A site can easily acquire 10,000 links through private blog networks (PBNs) or spammy directories for a few hundred dollars. In the short term, search algorithms may rank the site. Within months, however, manual actions or core spam updates will wipe out this ranking.

Require agencies to submit backlink audits for past clients. Inspect the Domain Authority distribution, the relevance of referring websites, and the ratio of branded to exact-match anchors.

3. Reporting parameters

Ensure your contracts specify transparent reporting parameters. Agencies must report on metrics that directly impact your bottom line: organic conversions, pipeline revenue, and non-branded visibility. If their monthly reports focus solely on total ranking keywords, search visibility percentages, or technical score percentages from automated tools, they are masking a lack of commercial business results.

Verification checklist

Isolated brand traffic — Ensure all case study metrics display non-branded organic clicks exclusively.

Link source verification — Audit backlink profile of references using external tools (Ahrefs, Semrush) to check for spam footprints.

Contractual SLAs on conversion — Tie agency compensation milestones to verified leads or organic transaction data rather than search volume.

Direct reference contact — Insist on calling past clients directly to verify project timelines and overall collaboration transparency.

By implementing these validation checks early in the procurement phase, you protect your digital investment and secure a partnership based on objective outcomes rather than polished slide decks.
